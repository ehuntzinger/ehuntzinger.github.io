About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at UC Davis. My past research encompasses everything from experimental heavy-ion nuclear physics to extragalactic observational astrophysics, which led me to my current research in the field of nuclear astrophysics. I study the astrophysical origin and production of r-process elements in the universe under the supervision of Dr. Erika Holmbeck (LLNL) and Dr. Stefano Valenti (UC Davis).

Prior to coming to UC Davis, I earned my B.S. in Physics at Lehigh University, where I studied di-hadron and jet-hadron collisions with Dr. Rosi Reed. At UC Davis, I initially worked under Dr. Manuel Calderón de la Barca Sánchez continuing my research in heavy-ion collisions before switching to observational astrophysics. I worked with Dr. Tucker Jones for two years in the field of galaxy formation and evolution, focusing on nebular oxygen abundances in star forming regions, prior to arriving at my current field of study.

On the more personal side, I am disabled, and my academic career has been shaped by the challenges presented by the inaccessibility of the academic landscape. I am a member of the Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI) committee in my department and my goal is that one day, there will be true equity in academia. I am passionate about creating a space where students are able to receive the support they need in order to achieve their goals.
