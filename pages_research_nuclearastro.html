The r- Process Production Ratio

My current research involves the rapid neutron capture process, or the r-process. The r-process is responsible for producing approximately half the natural abundance of elements heavier than Fe56 in the universe, with the natural abundance of elements heavier than bismuth being produced solely by the r-process, and is thought to occur primarily in neutron star mergers (NSMs). However, while r-process production in NSMs was confirmed in 2017 with the observations of GW170817 and its associated light curve, it remains unknown if NSMs and compact object mergers are in fact the primary source of the observed r-process elements in our Solar System and the universe.

I am currently investigating the origin of elements produced by the r-process by studying their abundances in stars in the Milky Way disk and halo in an attempt to put constraints on NSM r-process yields. I am using the radioactive decay of r-process elements observed in these stars as a means of obtaining an the "production ratio", the relative abundance of a radioactive r-process element to a stable r-process element at the time of its production. In other words, the relative abundance of r-process elements produced in NSMs.
